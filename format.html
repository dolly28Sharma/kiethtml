Soft landing simply means landing at a gentle, controlled speed to not sustain damage to a spacecraft.
Amitabha Ghosh, a scientist for NASA’s Rover mission to Mars,
explained it in The Indian Express thus:“Imagine a spacecraft hurtling through space,
at 10 times the speed of an airplane, having to nearly come to a standstill in order to land gently on the Earth
all in a matter of a few minutes and, more importantly, without any human intervention. This, in a nutshell, is a soft landing.”
